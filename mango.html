Mango Tree

Description:

  1. Mango trees are evergreen trees with a thick trunk and wide canopy.
  2. They can grow to a height of 100 feet or more with a canopy extending to about 35 feet or more, depending upon the climate and richness of the soil.
  3. The leaves are leathery, lanceolate, and found in simple-alternate arrangement on the branches.
  4. They are dark green and about 5â€“16 inches in length.
  5. Flowers are borne in panicles 4â€“16 inches long and have several hundred small, white flowers that are 1/4-inch wide when fully open.
  6. Most of the flowers function as male flowers, but some are bisexual and form fruits after pollination.
  7. The mango is called the "King of Fruits" due to its creamy, rich taste and aromatic flavor.
  8. The fruit of the mango tree is a drupe that varies in size and shape with shades of red and yellow or dull green.
  9. The fruit can be oval, round, heart-shaped, kidney-shaped, or long and slender.
  10. It has a single flat, large seed with a surrounding fleshy layer.

HOW TO GROW?

  1. The mango tree is a long lived one.
  2. It should be planted at a minimum distance of 8 meters between two plants.
  3. Mango trees require full sunlight to fruit well. It will grow in lesser light but not fruit well or may not fruit at all.
  4. Grafted plants are planted as seed ones are not true to type.
  5. Good soil preperation is required.
  6. Staking is required for young plants.
  7. Planting is recommended in June.
  8. Pits of 1 meter width x 1 meter length x 1 meter deep should be taken.
  9. Top soil from these pits should be mixed with 50 kgs farm yard manure / well decomposed cow dung manure, 2 kgs of bone meal, 2 kgs of single super phosphate and 50 gms follidol dust.
  10. Mix evenly to ensure proper distribution of the manure, fertilizer and pesticide.
  11. Watering can be done every 10 to 15 days in winter and at weekly intervals during summer.
  12. Get your plants from reliable sources. The cost of each dozen of mangoes is more than the plant cost.
